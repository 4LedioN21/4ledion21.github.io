PRIZREN

Prizren is an attractive Balkan city that gives the impression of flowing along the narrow bed of the Bistrica River, which divides it in half. Situated among the high green mountains, and a city built for centuries with a mixed architecture but clearly dominated by Ottoman influence, Prizren is undoubtedly one of the most attractive cities in Kosovo. Moreover, due to the organization of many summer festivals, and the fact that it is one of the most beautiful centers in all of Kosovo, Prizren has been named the cultural capital of Kosovo.

Prizren has everything it needs to make it an interesting destination for travelers both for fun and for business. The old town with the youngest population is a great place to visit, whether for a short drive from Pristina or Skopje to the Albanian coast, or for a long trip to see all the wonderful mountain surroundings. The beautiful center of Prizren is the best preserved and most picturesque part of Kosovo, with a good collection of religious buildings and special residential neighborhoods all within a short radius of each other. The early tradition of the city for Religious and ethnic tolerance is evident from the proximity of the Catholic and Orthodox churches, mosques and tekkes, most of which are several hundred years old. A full day enables you to see most of the things described here.

Prizren League House

-The Monumental Complex of the Albanian League of Prizren is a museum complex and cultural heritage monument located in the historic city of Prizren, Kosovo.

Prizren Castle

-from where the whole city is seen, it seems to have been built to protect and control the city, and already offers a magnificent view.

Sinan Pasha Mosque

-The Sinan Pasha Mosque is an Ottoman mosque in the city of Prizren, Kosovo. It was built in 1615 by Sofi Sinan Pasha, bey of Budim.The mosque overlooks the main street of Prizren and is a dominant feature in the town's skyline.
